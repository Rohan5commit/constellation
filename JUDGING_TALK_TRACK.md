# Judging Talk Track

## Solution Quality

- Constellation solves a clear and relatable problem: meaningful fragments get lost through fragmentation, not deletion.
- The prototype turns a vague pain into concrete flows: capture, cluster, rediscover, reconstruct, decide.
- The seeded demo shows clear user value in under a minute.

## Originality

- The product reframes Lost & Found around scattered digital memory rather than lost objects.
- The constellation metaphor is not cosmetic; it structures the interaction model and the narrative.
- The experience is distinct from generic AI productivity or note-taking tools.

## User Understanding

- The repository includes a visible research plan, provisional personas, JTBD framing, and usability templates.
- The concept is grounded in real behaviors: over-saving, context loss, and missed reconnection.
- Research artifacts clearly indicate where real participant evidence should be inserted.

## Design Iteration

- The process documents reframing, concept exploration, prioritization, and a planned validation loop.
- The usability plan identifies specific tasks, success criteria, and likely revision priorities.
- The MVP scope is intentionally focused for a designathon: fewer features, stronger narrative, higher polish.

## Interface Quality

- The visual system is calm, premium, and thematically cohesive.
- Every screen is designed for five-second comprehension.
- Motion supports rediscovery and emotional pacing rather than distracting spectacle.

## Theme Alignment

- The first screen makes Lost & Found immediately legible.
- The entire product is about helping users recover what was scattered, forgotten, or contextless.
- The emotional payoff is explicit: not storing more, but finding what still matters.
