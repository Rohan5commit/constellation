# Usability Test Plan

**Status:** Template for real usability validation. Insert findings after testing.

## Test Objectives

- confirm whether the product premise is understandable within seconds
- test whether users interpret constellations correctly
- assess whether resurfacing feels helpful or unsettling
- identify any confusion in the action / reconnect decision points

## Suggested Tasks

### Task 1: Interpret the Homepage
Prompt: “Without clicking anything yet, tell me what you think this product is for.”

**Success criteria**
- participant mentions recovering forgotten fragments, ideas, or moments
- participant recognizes the Lost & Found framing

### Task 2: Enter the Demo and Explain the Feed
Prompt: “Walk through the main prototype screen and explain what you think is happening.”

**Success criteria**
- participant can identify resurfaced fragments and understand why they are featured
- participant sees this as rediscovery, not just note storage

### Task 3: Open a Constellation
Prompt: “Choose a constellation and tell me why you think these fragments belong together.”

**Success criteria**
- participant understands the grouping logic without heavy explanation
- participant can describe an emotional or contextual theme

### Task 4: React to a Resurfaced Fragment
Prompt: “Open a fragment and decide what you would do next.”

**Success criteria**
- participant understands “why this resurfaced” explanation
- participant can choose a next step confidently

### Task 5: Evaluate the Reconnect Flow
Prompt: “Read this reconnect suggestion. Does it feel useful, intrusive, or something else?”

**Success criteria**
- participant interprets the suggestion as gentle rather than creepy
- participant understands the product is proposing, not insisting

## What To Observe

- hesitation before key actions
- confusion about the constellation metaphor
- trust reactions to AI language
- whether users can explain the value proposition in their own words
- whether any screen feels overloaded or too sparse
