# User Personas

**Status:** Provisional personas based on likely target users. Validate with real interviews before final judging.

## Persona 1: Alina, the Over-Saving Student Creator

- **Age:** 20
- **Context:** UCI student, design club member, side projects everywhere
- **Behavior:** screenshots references, saves internship links, bookmarks articles, records voice thoughts while walking to class
- **Current frustration:** remembers saving things, but not where or why
- **Emotional state:** inspired often, overwhelmed later

### Goals

- stop losing promising ideas to digital clutter
- reconnect notes, inspiration, and opportunities into something actionable
- feel less guilty about unfinished intentions

### Pain Points

- too many screenshots with no remembered context
- inspiration spread across notes, tabs, DMs, and voice memos
- task tools feel too rigid for half-formed ideas

### What Success Looks Like

A system that gently says: “these fragments belong together and they still mean something.”

## Persona 2: Daniel, the Thoughtful Early-Career Generalist

- **Age:** 25
- **Context:** first full-time job, still building side interests and relationships
- **Behavior:** saves podcast snippets, articles, gift ideas, message drafts, and career links
- **Current frustration:** keeps capturing fragments but only rediscovers them by accident
- **Emotional state:** busy, reflective, slightly disconnected from things he meant to return to

### Goals

- bring back ideas when they are actually relevant
- remember people and plans without turning life into a task list
- reduce the sense that meaningful things are constantly slipping away

### Pain Points

- reminders feel too blunt
- note apps preserve content but not significance
- relationship-related fragments disappear fastest because they are emotional, not urgent

### What Success Looks Like

A calm rediscovery tool that surfaces the right fragment with enough context to help him act, reconnect, or let go.
