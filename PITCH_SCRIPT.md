# Pitch Script

People do not just lose files. They lose the meaning attached to them.

A screenshot of a recipe. A note from a hard week. A link for an internship. A voice memo about a startup idea. A reminder to text a friend back. We save these fragments because they matter in the moment, but our digital lives scatter them so completely that the intention disappears.

That is the Lost & Found problem we designed for.

**Constellation** is an AI-assisted space for recovering forgotten ideas, moments, and connections from scattered digital fragments before they disappear.

Instead of behaving like another notes app, Constellation treats each saved fragment as a drifting star. When enough related fragments appear, the product groups them into constellations like *Unfinished Futures*, *People To Return To*, or *Quiet Signals*. The goal is not just storage. It is rediscovery.

In the demo, a user enters a rediscovery feed where forgotten fragments resurface based on unfinished intent, emotional weight, and related context. They can open a constellation to see why fragments belong together, then click into one resurfaced item to understand why it came back now.

From there, Constellation helps the user decide what to do next: act on an idea, reconnect with a person, archive something, or simply let it go. For people-related fragments, the reconnect flow suggests low-pressure prompts like a short message instead of demanding action.

We used AI carefully. NVIDIA NIM only supports moments where intelligence creates real value: clustering fragments, recovering likely context, drafting reflection prompts, and suggesting gentle next steps. The product never overclaims certainty and never invents memories that are not present in the data.

The design reasoning is simple: people are not overwhelmed because they save too much. They are overwhelmed because their fragments stop belonging to a coherent story. So we designed a calm, emotionally legible system that makes scattered memory feel findable again.

Constellation matters now because our digital lives are full of traces that once mattered to us. Lost & Found, in this context, is not about retrieving objects. It is about recovering what still deserves our attention.
