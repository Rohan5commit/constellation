# Research Plan

**Status:** Preparatory research artifact. Replace template sections with real participant evidence before final judging.

## Research Goals

1. Understand how people currently lose track of meaningful digital fragments.
2. Determine whether users describe the pain as disorganization, overload, forgetting, guilt, or missed opportunity.
3. Validate whether “rediscovery” is more compelling than “organization” as a product frame.
4. Test whether users perceive constellation-based grouping as helpful and intuitive.
5. Identify the trust boundary for AI-generated resurfacing and reconnection prompts.

## Key Hypotheses

- Users feel more frustrated by **context loss** than by storage limitations.
- People save fragments with intention, but rarely revisit them with that intention intact.
- Gentle resurfacing will feel more useful than aggressive productivity reminders.
- People are more open to AI grouping than AI summarizing if the language stays uncertain and transparent.
- A visually calm, reflective interface will outperform a dashboard-style interface for this problem space.

## Target Participants

Recruit 5 to 7 participants who match at least one of the following:

- college students juggling projects, deadlines, and social commitments
- creative students who save inspiration across many apps
- early-career professionals who screenshot, bookmark, and voice-note constantly
- people who self-identify as “I save too much and rarely go back”

## Interview Guide

### Warm-up

- Tell me about the last thing you saved because it felt important.
- Where do meaningful fragments usually end up for you?

### Current Behavior

- What kinds of things do you screenshot, bookmark, or write down?
- Which tools do you currently use for notes, reminders, links, or voice thoughts?
- How often do you intentionally revisit saved fragments?

### Pain and Loss

- When does a saved fragment become effectively lost for you?
- What types of fragments feel most painful to lose track of?
- Have you ever missed an opportunity because you forgot something you had already saved?

### Reflection and Meaning

- Do you usually remember why you saved something later?
- What kinds of context would help a forgotten fragment make sense again?
- How would you want a product to bring something back without feeling annoying or creepy?

### Reaction to Concept

- If your saved fragments were grouped into “constellations,” what would you expect that to mean?
- How would you feel about AI saying “this might still matter” versus “you should do this now”?
- For people-related fragments, what would make a reconnect prompt feel acceptable?

## Testing Goals

During concept tests, observe whether participants can:

- explain the product value after the first screen
- understand why fragments are grouped together
- interpret the rediscovery feed correctly
- trust the “why this resurfaced” explanations
- understand the reconnect flow without feeling manipulated
